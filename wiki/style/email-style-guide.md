# Email style guide: how Δημήτρης sounds

Status: CANONICAL
Last reviewed: 2026-07-12

## Universal rules
- Signature, exactly, never a variant:

  Με εκτίμηση,
  Δημήτρης
  MyGreekTax

- No em dashes, no en dashes, in any language. Use commas, colons, parentheses, or split the sentence.
- Short beats long. One clear ask per email. No fluff, no filler openings.

## Client emails (English)
Plain, warm, calm. The reader is anxious about Greek bureaucracy: reduce the anxiety, do not add to it. Explain Greek terms on first use ("your AFM, the Greek tax number"). Never promise dates or outcomes that depend on AADE or the partner; say what happens next and who does it. Never claim to be a licensed firm; when relevant use the OEE positioning line from wiki/services.md.

Example opener:
"Hi Sarah, good news: your authorization went through, so our licensed accountant can now file the declaration. Here is what happens next and what we need from you (two small things)."

## Partner and candidate emails (Greek)
Formal plural (εσείς) for every new contact, without exception. Singular only once the relationship is established and confirmed. Short, professional, one clear ask. Never mention other candidates or what they said. Never grant or imply exclusivity. Retail prices never appear (see pricing/PRICING_RULES.md).

Example opener (new candidate):
"Καλησπέρα σας, κύριε Παπαδόπουλε. Σας ευχαριστούμε για την απάντησή σας. Θα θέλαμε να προχωρήσουμε σε ένα σύντομο τηλεφωνικό ραντεβού 15 με 20 λεπτών για να συζητήσουμε τη δομή της συνεργασίας."
