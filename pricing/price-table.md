## Correction: AFM registration is NOT negative margin

Earlier this table wrongly compared the bare AFM-number retail (€39) against the full-Setup wholesale (€45). Those are two different services. Corrected:

- **AFM Basic (number only)**, e.g. a remote property buyer who needs an AFM and nothing else: retail €39 list, wholesale €30, margin €9 (23%). Thin but positive.
- **AFM + Setup** (AFM, κλειδάριθμος, TAXISnet and myAADE activation), the relocating client: actually quoted at €99 (Q-03), wholesale €45, margin €54 (55%). The €70 figure (Q-07) was a legacy first-client rate and is retired; do not reuse it for new leads.

So the typical AFM service (Setup) runs a healthy margin. There is no loss on this line.

## The real underwater line: Tax Authority Letters

The rate card lists "Tax Authority Letter response" at €20. Chrysostomos wholesale for the same work (translation plus drafting a reply to an AADE letter) is "από €25 βασικό", case by case. So the list retail sits at least €5 below wholesale before any of your own time. This is the line that actually needs a pricing decision: raise the retail floor to at least €49, or always quote it case by case rather than off a €20 list price.

- Revisit any line where wholesale exceeds roughly 60% of retail. On confirmed figures that flags only Tax Authority Letters and, marginally, E2 once several rental lines stack up.
